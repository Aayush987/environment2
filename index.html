ENVIRONMENT

Environment is the influencing circumstances, objects, and conditions of an area. Factors contributing to the environment are physical, chemical, biotic, social, and cultural. The term environment is derived from the middle French preposition 'environ' meaning 'around' or 'that which surrounds'. The primary uses of the word are used to refer to the physical environment or geographical environment, such as 'electric vehicle sales are increasing because people care about the environment' or 'Jane Goodall loves to observe chimpanzees in their natural environment', and to refer to the social/cultural environment or man-made environment , such as 'we are trying to create a better environment within the company' or 'the child was raised in a loving environment'.

GEOGRAPHICAL ENVIRONMENT

Geographical environment is the terrestrial environment that is a creation of complex natural and environmental conditions. Although it arose independently of humankind, it is the complement of direct interaction between nature and human society. The geographical environment revolves around the subjects of climatology, geology, biogeography, etc. They are direct aspects of how human society conceptualizes the geography of the earth. A geographical environment is also called a natural environment as it interacts with nature.
